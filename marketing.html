The Narrative

There resides a tree skeleton in the palms of our hands—that’s what I grew up believing. When I pressed my palms together, I saw branches in those scattered lines. An old tree. Leafless. Waiting. I used to wonder if winter was the only season in human life.


I drew suns in blue, flowers in impossible shapes, and forests inspired by 'রুশদেশের উপকথা'. A single word could unfold an entire universe in my mind. I would spend hours narrating those imaginary worlds to my mother—much to her exhaustion. Creation, for me, was never an activity. It was instinct.


Painting taught me flow. Music taught me rhythm. Even leaving pickles to dry on the rooftop taught me patience, and gratitude for air, for time, for process. That is how I understand brands.


I don’t see products. I see seasons. I see roots. I see the silent winters before a spring. Marketing, to me, is not persuasion—it is interpretation. It is finding the story already living beneath the surface and giving it language.


And here I am, translating heartbeats into narratives people recognize as their own.
